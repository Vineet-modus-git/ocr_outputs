Other income includes (i) insurance claim received; (ii) reversal of impairment allowance for trade receivables; (iii) profit on sale of property, plant & equipment (net); (iv) export incentives; (v) liabilities no longer required written back; and (vi) miscellaneous income.

## Expenses

Our total expenses comprise (i) cost of raw material and components consumed; (ii) purchase of traded goods; (iii) construction material and contract expenses; (iv) (increase)/decrease in inventories of finished goods, work-in-progress and traded goods; (v) employee benefits expense; (vi) depreciation and amortisation expense; (vii) finance costs; (vii) finance income; and (viii) other expenses.

### Cost of raw material and components consumed

Cost of raw material and components consumed comprises of inventory at the beginning of the year and purchases during the year less inventory at the end of the year.

### Purchase of traded goods

Purchase of traded goods simply comprises of the traded goods purchased for the purpose of resale to customers.

### Construction material and contract expenses

Construction material and contract expenses comprises (i) construction material; and (ii) subcontracting charges.

### (Increase)/decrease in inventories of finished goods, work-in-progress and traded goods

(Increase)/decrease in inventories of finished goods, work-in-progress and traded goods is the difference between the (I) opening inventories which includes (i) traded goods; (ii) work-in-progress and (iii) finished goods; and (II) closing inventories which includes (i) traded goods; (ii) work-in-progress; and (iii) finished goods.

### Employee benefits expense

Employee benefits expense comprises (i) salaries, wages and bonus; (ii) contribution to provident, superannuation and other employee welfare funds; (iii) share based payment expense; (iv) gratuity expense and (v) staff welfare expenses.

### Depreciation and amortisation expense

Depreciation and amortisation expense comprises (i) depreciation of property, plant and equipment; (ii) depreciation of right-of-use assets and (iii) amortisation of intangible assets.

### Finance costs

Finance costs primarily comprises: (i) interest on financial liabilities measured at amortised cost; (ii) bill discounting and factoring charges; (iii) dividend paid on non-cumulative redeemable preference shares; (iv) bank charges; and (v) interest on lease liabilities.

### Finance income

Finance income comprises (i) interest income on (a) bank deposits; (b) loans given to related parties; and (c) income tax refund; (ii) gain on sale of mutual funds; (iii) fair value gain on financial instruments measured at fair value through profit and loss.

### Other expenses

Other expenses comprises (i) consumption of stores and spares; (ii) power, fuel and water; (iii) repairs and maintenance including (a) building and (b) machinery; (iv) service expenses and labour charges; (v) consumption of packing materials; (vi) sales commission; (vii) carriage outwards; (viii) rent; (ix) insurance; (x) rates and taxes; (xi) travelling and conveyance; (xii) legal and professional fees; (xiii) advertisement and business promotion expenses; (xiv) loss on sale of property, plant & equipment (net); (xv) network maintenance charges; (xvi) directors sitting fee and commission; (xvii) bad debts written off; (xviii) impairment allowance for trade receivables; and (xix) miscellaneous expenses.
